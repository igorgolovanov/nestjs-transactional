## Conventions surfaced

- **Date discipline rule** (mid-flight in commit 3, recorded in
  user-side memory). The standard ADR header `**Date**: YYYY-MM-DD`
  field is repo convention (matches ADR-001..016) and stays.
  Inline body dates ("Phase 14.3 (2026-04-26) shipped...") and
  Revision-history bullets in the form `- **2026-04-27** (Phase X)
  — ...` are noisy and avoided in favour of phase-anchored bullets
  (`- **Phase 14.20** — § 9 added: ...`). The phase numbers carry
  the chronology; the doubled-up date+phase prefix in bullets adds
  no information. Filenames and content of `docs/status/*` and
  `docs/sessions/*` retain their dates because those documents are
  explicit chronological retrospectives — the date IS the filing
  scheme.

  The rule was triggered by the user's reaction to the initial
  Revision history format (`**2026-04-27** (Phase 14.0–14.3) —
  original ADR drafted...`) in commit 3, and refined when the
  user pointed out that ADR-018/019 should still match the
  ADR-001..016 header convention. Memory entry
  `feedback_no_dates_in_files.md` captures the nuanced version.
  Applied across commit 3 ADRs, commit 4 migration guide, and
  commit 5 roadmap.

## Design observations

- **Doc sweep verified architectural coherence.** Five commits
  touched 48 files across READMEs / ADRs / guides / roadmap; the
  fact that the addendum collapse (commit 3) and the roadmap
  restructure (commit 5) didn't surface a conflict between
  shipped state and any single ADR Decision suggests the
  underlying architecture has stabilised. Phase 14.20 / 14.21
  were the last architectural extensions; subsequent work is
  example library and documentation alignment.

- **Cross-references-as-glue scaled.** The Phase 14.8 example
  library now serves as the runnable reference for every
  package README, the migration guide, both ADRs, and the
  roadmap. Authoring guidance going forward: when documenting
  a feature, link to the smallest example demonstrating it AND
  to the largest example exercising it in production-realism
  context. Tier 1 (foundational) + Tier 5 (`e-commerce-orders`
  flagship) is the typical pair.

- **Net deletion is a healthy signal at era closure.** -683 LoC
  across the corpus (insertions minus deletions) at the close of
  the multi-adapter era. The pre-tier examples and the ADR
  addendums had served their purpose during the era's
  development; their retirement is part of consolidating the
  era's legacy into a coherent final form. Future doc sweeps
  closing later eras should aim for a similar shape — net
  reduction with structural clarity gain.

Variance pattern:

- Insertion side hit the lower bound (1612 vs envelope 1500-2500).
  Reason: the audit anticipated heavier expansion in commit 4
  (migration guide) and commit 5 (roadmap) than what materialised.
  Commit 4 settled at +293 because much of the existing structure
  worked; the rewrite tightened code snippets and added two
  sections (Multi-DS, Externalization) without rewriting the
  framework verbatim. Commit 5 settled at +716 — large but the
  net was only +16 because the era-based structure replaced
  equivalent chronological text.
- Deletion side significantly above envelope upper bound (2295 vs
  envelope 800-1500). Reason: commit 2 retired two complete
  example folders (21 files); the audit anticipated this but
  underestimated the per-folder line count (combined ~1000 LoC
  source + READMEs across 21 files). Future "retire stale
  examples" estimates should anchor on the per-example baseline
  established here (~50 LoC per source file × ~10 files per
  folder = 500 LoC per folder).
- Net contraction (-683) was not on the original audit dimension.
  An era-closure doc sweep can plausibly contract the corpus on
  net; that's a healthy sign rather than a budgetary surprise,
  and the audit should have framed the deletion side with the
  expectation that "retire pre-tier" alone would carry it.
